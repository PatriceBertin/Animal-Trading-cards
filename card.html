Cape Robin-Chat

The Cape Robin-Chat is a small passerine bird found in southern Africa. They are known for their distinctive appearance, characterized by dark brown or black upperparts, a white belly, and a prominent white stripe above the eye.

  • Scientific Name: Cossypha caffra
  • Size: 17-19cm
  • Lifespan: 2-3years
  • Habital: forests ,woodlands, gardens, scrublands

Cape Robin-Chats are skilled insect hunters and forage for small invertebrates in leaf litter and vegetation. They are also renowned for their melodious songs, often heard during the breeding season. Overall, Cape Robin-Chats are adaptable and common birds, appreciated for their beauty and song.
